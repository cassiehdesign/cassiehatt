Complete Brand Guidelines

Logo Concept

When creating the logo for The Open Box, it was very important to find the right balance between a fun and friendly logo, and one that is still clean and professional. The focus is less on the cafe aspect and more on the essence of a boardgame on a table. With this concept, the container acts as the table but also helps contain the logo since the text is more playful. The customized font is bold and chunky to be recognizable from a distance, and angling the letters gives the logo movement while representing how a boardgame would be set up on a table. Incorporating the cutout elements of the meeple in the E and X is a subtle way to add another boardgame aspect to the logo. With the use of the dropdown shadow, the meeple head in the X is duplicated, which symbolizes playing a game with others. With all of these elements put together, it creates a cohesive logo that represents The Open Box and what you can expect when entering.

Primary Logo

The primary logo is the main identifier that people will associate with The Open Box. This logo needs to convey the feel and encompass what The Open Box is like. People should get a sense of what to expect when entering the cafe just from the logo itself. This is the logo that will appear in most places such as the storefront and stationary.

Logo Limitations

It is important to follow the guide of logo limitations to ensure that the logo is the same across all usages. This includes ensuring the logo has the same dimensions and colours anywhere that it appears to keep recognizability high.

  1. Do not distort the logo in any way
  2. Do not change the colours from those of the brand or add gradients to the logo
  3. Do not reverse the colours of the logo
  4. Do not change the font of the logo
  5. Do not place the logo on a busy background
  6. Do not rearrange the elements of the logo

Font Families

Using the same fonts across all platforms keeps the brand unified and looks the best to those who see it. The header font is similar to that used in the logo, this gives a sense of cohesion. It is bold and playful but still very legible, the perfect choice to use in header elements.

The copy font, Verdana, is a very clean and legible font which is ideal for a copy font. It balances very well with the header font and also includes a bold version which can be used. Verdana translates well from a screen to print, so it can be used in all areas. Anton Regular is the perfect supporting font, it is like a fusion of the header and copy fonts. This font is perfect for subheaders and areas that need some more separation between the header and copy.

Colour Palette

It is important to always use the brand colours across all areas. This keeps the brand cohesive and ensures all elements such as the logo and assets will always match. The colours chosen are all complementary to each other and provide great contrast between themselves and the primary logo colours.

Assets

Assets are a great supplementary way of adding to designs and the brand. They vary from illustrated elements to patterns that can be used across various platforms. The illustrated assets focus on boardgame elements that can be used in the background of social posts, on the website, in the cafe, etc. The pattern is a combination of all assets and is the perfect fun way to use throughout areas.
